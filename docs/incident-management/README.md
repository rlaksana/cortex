# Incident Management System

## Overview

This comprehensive incident management system provides structured workflows, templates, and procedures for effective incident detection, response, coordination, and learning. The system is designed to minimize business impact, ensure consistent response quality, and drive continuous improvement across the organization.

## üìã Document Structure

This incident management system consists of the following components:

### 1. [On-Call Roster and Triage Timeline](./on-call-roster-and-triage.md)
- **Purpose**: Define on-call schedules, triage procedures, and escalation protocols
- **Key Features**:
  - On-call rotation management and handover procedures
  - Triage decision trees and priority matrices
  - Escalation paths and contact information
  - Performance metrics and continuous improvement

### 2. [Communication Templates](./communication-templates.md)
- **Purpose**: Provide standardized communication templates for all audiences
- **Key Features**:
  - Internal communication templates (technical, management, resolution)
  - External communication templates (customers, partners, regulatory)
  - Social media and press release templates
  - Communication planning worksheets and checklists

### 3. [Post-Mortem Templates and Procedures](./post-mortem-templates.md)
- **Purpose**: Establish structured post-incident review processes
- **Key Features**:
  - Executive summary and detailed technical templates
  - Lightweight and quick retrospective formats
  - Meeting structure and documentation procedures
  - Action item management and quality assurance

### 4. [Severity Classification System](./severity-classification.md)
- **Purpose**: Define consistent incident severity classification (SEV-1 to SEV-4)
- **Key Features**:
  - Detailed severity definitions and impact criteria
  - Response time objectives and escalation triggers
  - Classification decision trees and procedures
  - Impact assessment frameworks and examples

### 5. [Response Coordination Procedures](./response-coordination.md)
- **Purpose**: Establish incident command structure and coordination protocols
- **Key Features**:
  - Incident command roles and responsibilities
  - Cross-functional team coordination procedures
  - War room setup and management (physical and virtual)
  - External communication procedures and media protocols

### 6. [Learning and Improvement Methodologies](./learning-and-improvement.md)
- **Purpose**: Create frameworks for learning from incidents and driving improvements
- **Key Features**:
  - Root cause analysis methodologies (5 Whys, Fishbone, Timeline)
  - Knowledge management systems and learning communities
  - Action item management and implementation frameworks
  - Measurement systems and continuous feedback loops

## üöÄ Quick Start Guide

### For New Team Members

1. **Read the Severity Classification System** first to understand how incidents are categorized
2. **Review the On-Call Roster procedures** to understand your role in incident response
3. **Familiarize yourself with Communication Templates** for your role and responsibilities
4. **Study Response Coordination procedures** to understand team dynamics
5. **Review Post-Mortem templates** to understand documentation requirements
6. **Understand Learning and Improvement methodologies** to contribute to continuous improvement

### For Incident Commanders

1. **During Incident**:
   - Use Severity Classification for incident categorization
   - Follow Response Coordination procedures for team management
   - Utilize Communication Templates for stakeholder updates
   - Document decisions and actions for post-mortem

2. **Post-Incident**:
   - Conduct Post-Mortem using provided templates
   - Identify improvement opportunities
   - Update processes based on lessons learned
   - Share knowledge with the broader organization

### For Managers and Leaders

1. **Review Regularly**:
   - Severity Classification accuracy and trends
   - Response time metrics and compliance
   - Communication effectiveness
   - Post-Mortem quality and completeness
   - Improvement implementation progress

2. **Support Your Team**:
   - Ensure proper training and resources
   - Remove organizational barriers
   - Support blameless learning culture
   - Celebrate learning and improvements
   - Provide adequate time for learning activities

## üìä Key Performance Indicators

### Incident Response Metrics
- **Mean Time to Detection (MTTD)**: Average time from incident occurrence to detection
- **Mean Time to Acknowledgment (MTTA)**: Average time from detection to team acknowledgment
- **Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR)**: Average time from detection to resolution
- **Incident Recurrence Rate**: Percentage of incidents that recur within 90 days

### Process Quality Metrics
- **Severity Classification Accuracy**: Percentage of incidents correctly classified initially
- **Post-Mortem Completion Rate**: Percentage of incidents with completed post-mortems
- **Action Item Completion Rate**: Percentage of improvement actions completed on time
- **Communication Compliance**: Percentage of communications sent within required timeframes

### Learning and Improvement Metrics
- **Knowledge Sharing Effectiveness**: Number of team members accessing incident knowledge
- **Improvement Implementation Success**: Percentage of implemented improvements that prevent recurrence
- **Training Effectiveness**: Assessment scores from incident response training
- **Culture Indicators**: Survey results on blameless culture and psychological safety
